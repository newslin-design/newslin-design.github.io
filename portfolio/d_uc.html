Marketing Research

In order to gain a deeper understanding of live streaming,
I explored its essence and define the key components.

I conducted extensive research on various types of webcasting and categorized them accordingly. Taking into account the historical development and recent changes in the industry, we identified distinct stages and outlined the unique characteristics associated with each stage.
Through our analysis, we discovered that two key technical factors play a crucial role in the webcasting industry: the level of interaction and equipment expertise. Based on these factors, we found that the market offers different solutions catering to varying needs.
Furthermore, our research revealed an interesting trend in the development process. Almost all types of live streaming are evolving towards enhanced interactivity and improved broadcast quality for several reasons, exemplified by the Program Live Streaming category depicted in the diagram.

The Changing Landscape of Live Streaming: Unveiling Three Key Trends

The three types of change trends in Streaming Live By converting the development process into four quadrants, we can see the trend flow more clearly, respectively; the old media want to increase its channels to broad participation or interaction by the internet. Problem discovery and solution

From the targeted users in the above scenario,
we have identified pain points related to equipment set-up and live operation.

Q1. Difficulty in hardware installation

The number of devices is exponentially increasing, and different video and audio sources require different cables and adapter devices, so setting up the devices will require more professional knowledge. In addition, the number of pieces of equipment connected is easy to make mistakes during live streaming.

Q2. Lack of professional staff and knowledge to do an excellent job of program content

To make good programming, it needs professional functions and knowledge of the broadcasting industry, as well as the support of experienced staff such as directors, sound controllers, and graphic stuff

Q3. Don't know how to LIVE where you want to be

This is the responsibility of the Master control in the traditional broadcasting industry, controlling its many channels, which requires a great deal of professional knowledge.

Q1. Difficulty in hardware installation :

Integration of a large number of professional hardware equipment

Professional live streaming requires many devices; we work with the PM to develop a corresponding i/o to complete all functions in one machine, significantly reducing the difficulty of connecting devices. Take the following picture as an example of a music teaching situation. Initially, many devices were needed, including Video capturers, Audio mixers, Audio interfaces, Controler, etc., and professional hardware knowledge was required. Q2. Lack of professional staff and knowledge to do an excellent job of program content

1. Easy but professional directing function

Make the professional director's preview and switching function easier, allowing users to easily switch and monitor each screen through their iPad or computer.

2. Converted programs to broadcast level programs

Innovative scene-based operations reduce the need for streaming live workforce to achieve broadcast-quality programming. Easy to use word cards and complete multi-camera screen processing.

3. Simplify audio mixer functionality and add automated features

Intuitive user interface with many defaults for novice users and features for advanced users. For directors, we provide an audio-follow-video function to reduce the workload

Q3. Don't know how to LIVE where you want to be:

Offers multiple easy-to-use streaming channelss

Integrate common live streaming channels and stream them through a simple UI, including support for Oauth, Stream key RTDP, and common online platforms, and also help customize live streaming to other platforms
