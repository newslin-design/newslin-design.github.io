Background

From Windows Phone and Android smartphones to VR, HTC has always been a technological leader in the industry. What could be the next step for HTC in the future? This plan is considering how to use HTC's current brand value and product line to think about the next possible direction. In the past two years, many companies that started with software have been moving towards hardware development, such as Microsoft's Surface series and Google's various innovative products. It is evident that in today's era of flourishing software development, the hardware devices that support this software will become increasingly important. HTC, starting from ODM, also maintains certain advantages in production. Currently, in many fields, the application of robots is being discussed. Consumer robots like Zenbo and Pepper are products where artificial intelligence can independently assist human life. However, HTC could combine the advantages of smartphones and VR to develop another possibility: Viver, positioned as another body for users. It can use the powerful interactive features of VIVE to remotely operate robots and interact with the environment and people in another domain. Product Positioning

Remote control robots used for providing images, sound, screen, and interactive movement. Offering real-time VR interactive experiences for remote education and tourism. Introducing a new concept of transportation - replacing physical movement with the movement of consciousness and senses.

Market Positioning/Target Group

[Trend of technology entering the education market] Education and learning have always been crucial to human progress. In recent years, various technological advancements have been introduced into the classroom education market, updating education models that haven't changed much in hundreds of years. From the free online open courses (Open Course MOOCs) initiated by MIT in 2002 to this year's Google Classroom and Microsoft's Surface Laptop designed for the education market, it's evident that the education market is undergoing a prolonged technological revolution, with VR not missing from this revolution. [Target Group] Educational institutions, universities, high schools, and other educational groups. [Problems Viver aims to solve] Unrealistic, non-immersive, and low interactive remote teaching experiences. The cost of time and money for remote education, such as cross-national academic teaching, architecture, exhibitions. Remote teaching in dangerous or hard-to-reach environments, such as mountain area guide training, volcanic academic research, driving training.

Design

On-site visual/auditory experiences in remote areas.

Accompanying VR equipment with camera and sound recording devices. Viver's head can move left, right, up, down, forward, and backward, allowing remote VR users to feel immersive and letting those interacting with Viver sense the VR user's head posture. E.g., leaning forward, nodding.

Interactive screen

Interactive screen for enhanced interaction between Viver and remote users.

New experiences in remote free movement.

Equipped with wheels for movement, allowing the VR user to move Viver and interact with space. The current model is for flat terrain; for rugged or uneven surfaces, the lower part can be replaced with different wheels.

Usage Scenarios in the Target Market

In the educational/academic research market, Viver can be used for forest ecology tours/teaching with one Viver for multiple students. Viver can serve students/public who need remote viewing at museums, exhibition halls, research sites, and fixed educational venues. It can be one student per Viver, or one Viver for multiple students using the Vive room, where the teacher controls and explains in real-time, interacting with space through Viver.
